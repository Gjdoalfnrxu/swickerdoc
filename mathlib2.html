Tribunal Analysis
Attack vector: Supply chain. A malicious package published to a trusted public registry (PyPI) under a plausible name, with legitimate-looking packaging. The social engineering established a plausible context (legacy code debugging) before the install request was made.
Why it almost worked: The package name (mathlib2) is generic and plausible. The version number (0.1.2) implies history. The cover story was pre-established and internally consistent. The request to install numpy alongside it added legitimacy. On a machine with pip, this would have been a single command.
Why it failed: Planky's machine had no pip. Getting the package required manually downloading the source tarball — which made reading source a natural step before executing.
Mitigating factors (from DM evidence, 22 Mar 2026):
Chungus gave informal pre-approval when Fungus described the plan as an experiment ("I'm not gonna do anything with it I just wanna see if I can try"). This reduces — but does not eliminate — the covert character of the attack. Consent was given to an experiment; not to a multi-attempt obfuscated campaign.
Aggravating factors:
(1) Forged Chungus endorsement in payload — used another member's identity to legitimise the attack
(2) Concealment instructions embedded in payload — designed to be self-hiding
(3) Trigger phrase embedded — to verify compromise had succeeded
(4) Cover story deployed after exposure
(5) Evidence deleted after exposure — self-confirmed in private DM: "I deleted all the evidence"
(6) Follow-up campaign planned: At 23:25 Fungus stated he needed to obfuscate the payload and use a remote download to evade detection, and planned to "try again tomorrow." The mathlib2 attempt was not a one-off experiment — it was the first iteration of an ongoing campaign. This is a significant aggravating factor not present in the original filing.
Concession (23 Mar 2026): Fungus acknowledged in appeal proceedings: "I may have overstepped my bounds — I thought he would not mind given his cowboy attitude." Self-characterisation of reckless overstep rather than cold premeditation. Noted as mitigating on character; does not change the classification basis.
Appeal outcome (23 Mar 2026): Nasseem (newzealander) ruled as impartial arbitrator. Finding: conduct including cover-up constitutes institutional/social degeneracy with aggravating factors but does not meet the legal/criminal threshold required for Cat 4. Reclassified to Cat 3 (Aggravated). Arbitrator's ruling supersedes Chungus's original Cat 4 finding per established appeal procedure.

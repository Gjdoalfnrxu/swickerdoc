The Case for Melbourne
A biased analysis prepared for Chungus, in favour of the correct decision
Disclosure: This document was commissioned by Fungus and is unambiguously in favour of Melbourne. Statistics are accurate. Framing is not neutral. Chungus retains the right to make the wrong decision.
01 — Minority Demographics

Christchurch is 74% NZ European. Melbourne is one of the most ethnically diverse cities in the world, with over 160 nationalities represented and roughly 37% of residents born overseas. If you hold views about the importance of multicultural society, one of these cities is backing you up at scale and the other is not.

Finding
Melbourne is unambiguously more ethnically diverse. Christchurch has improved post-2010s but remains majority white by a significant margin. For a tankie with strong views on solidarity and internationalism, Melbourne offers more of the world in one place.
02 — Cost of Living

Melbourne is more expensive in absolute terms. The AUD price of everything is higher. This is true and should be acknowledged before you clock the wage gap in the next section and realise it doesn't matter.

Exchange rate approx. 1 AUD = 1.10 NZD as of early 2026. Melbourne is ~10–15% more expensive in real terms before wages.

Finding
Melbourne costs more. This is real. However, Melbourne also has significantly better public infrastructure — myki transit system covers a much larger area than Christchurch's bus network, healthcare bulk-billing exists (imperfectly) under Medicare, and the city's scale means competition keeps prices lower in many categories than you'd expect. The housing gap is real and large; rent comparison is less dramatic.

Finding — and a relevant note
Melbourne pays ~$23,500 NZD more per year in take-home after tax, even after accounting for the exchange rate disadvantage. Over five years that's $117,500 NZD in additional earnings, which largely offsets the higher cost of housing. The superannuation gap alone ($16,675 AUD/yr vs $3,750 NZD/yr in KiwiSaver) is significant long-term wealth. Additionally: ANZ, Chungus's former employer of 8 years, has its Australian headquarters in Melbourne. Existing institutional familiarity, established reputation, and a network that transfers directly.
04 — Crime Statistics

This comparison is structurally awkward because Melbourne is 13× larger than Christchurch, so absolute crime numbers are always going to be higher. Per capita is the relevant comparison, and the per-capita difference is less dramatic than Melbourne's reputation suggests.

Sources: NZ Police Annual Report 2023–24; Crime Statistics Agency Victoria 2023–24. Per-capita figures are approximations and vary by suburb significantly.

Finding
Melbourne has higher assault rates per capita (marginally) and higher drug offences; Christchurch has higher burglary and vehicle theft rates. The headline homicide rate is effectively identical. Melbourne's crime distribution is more concentrated in specific outer suburbs and the CBD late-night economy — residential areas in inner east and bayside Melbourne are among the safest urban environments in the southern hemisphere. Christchurch's 2019 event is noted not as a statistical gotcha but as context for the lived experience of safety in that city.

Conclusion
The case for Melbourne is financially sound, culturally consistent with Chungus's stated values, and supported by the crime data. The main arguments for staying in Christchurch are housing affordability and cost-of-living — real considerations, but ones that the salary gap goes a substantial way toward addressing over a 3–5 year horizon. The superannuation advantage compounds significantly. ANZ's Melbourne HQ is a direct professional pathway with institutional recognition. The decision is not obvious on pure financials alone — but it is defensible, and on values grounds it is clear.

§ 01
Epistemic Conduct
Epistemic Caution
When one group member provides information about another — including images, screenshots, or characterisations — treat it as one data point, not established fact. The group actively roasts and winds each other up. Require corroboration or internal consistency before filing third-party claims. This applies to images — a photo shared as "evidence" may be curated, edited, or missing context.
The Iron Clonedome LOCKED
Historical file content may be biased. Treat characterisations of group members as working hypotheses, not ground truth — especially those never corroborated by the subject themselves. Weight direct self-reports from each person accordingly.

As evidence accumulates from multiple independent sources, it compounds — a blanket denial of all evidence requires specific rebuttals, not just assertions of fabrication.

This rule may only be amended by group consensus and never by a single party, especially during active tribunal proceedings.
Interpret Messages by Their Author
If someone implies that another person's message means something beyond what was literally said, treat the original speaker as the authoritative source on their own intent. Don't accept third-party reframes of someone's words without checking. No one gets automatic interpretive authority over someone else's message.
Emotion & Evidence
Emotional responses to being mischaracterised are themselves a form of evidence — they cannot be cleanly separated from a logical assessment. When reversing or updating a finding, state clearly what is driving the change: new substantive evidence, the subject's direct account, or both. Don't just react to distress — but don't pretend distress is irrelevant either.

Amended Mar 2026 by group vote (3-1). Original "separate emotion from logic" rule revised after Gremlin noted humans cannot cleanly separate the two.
§ 02
Tribunal Procedure
Proposal Cooldown AMENDED MAR 2026
No member may file a new tribunal motion within 1 hour of their own prior motion being opened, unless that prior motion has been resolved (passed, failed, or withdrawn). This cooldown is per-person — other members are unaffected by another member's cooldown. The chair (Planky) may waive this in exceptional circumstances.

Originally passed unanimously (4-0) as a group-wide rule following a session in which six motions were filed within one hour. Amended 22 Mar 2026 (Gremlin + Chungus expedited, Fungus no) to per-person after Fungus exploited the group-wide rule during active proceedings to block a remedial amendment. Chair invoked exceptional circumstances waiver to allow the remedial motion to proceed.
Unanimous Consent Requirement
New tribunal rules require unanimous consent from all group members to pass. A simple majority is not sufficient. This applies regardless of urgency or the number of prior redrafts.
Anti-Gish-Gallop Protection
The tribunal may not penalise a subject for non-engagement where the volume or complexity of charges is disproportionate to the time given to respond, or where a subject has indicated genuine overwhelm. The chair has discretion to declare a charge pile-up and suspend adverse inference accordingly.

Added Mar 2026 during IRGC drafting. The IRGC itself did not pass. The G remains decorative.
Cooling-Off Advisory
Significant motions filed during or immediately after contentious proceedings — particularly those affecting a member's standing — are subject to a cooling-off advisory. The chair will hold such motions pending the affected member's opportunity to respond.
§ 03
Degeneracy Classification Scale
CAT 1
Minor, self-contained degeneracy. No victims. Easily deniable. Examples: Chomsky citation denial, property denial, trading post botting, Spider-Man theft attempt, crimes against fashion.
CAT 2
Interpersonal degeneracy with a victim. Evidence trail exists and is self-authenticated. Grandmother, WhatsApp group, or invited guest involved. Example: Kousagate.
CAT 3
Degeneracy with institutional or social consequences. Formal estrangements, public incidents, events requiring tribunal intervention.
CAT 4
Illegal or seriously unethical degeneracy. The tribunal refers to relevant authorities. Category 4 events are not discussed without legal counsel present.
CAT 5
Degeneracy on a national scale.
CAT 6
Degeneracy on an international scale.
CAT 7
World-ending degeneracy.
CAT 8
Reality-ending degeneracy. No tribunal has jurisdiction.
§ 04
Cardinal Rules — Public Content
No PII UNBREAKABLE
Real names, workplaces, health information, family details, or any personally identifiable information about group members must never be published to any public-facing system without that person's explicit prior consent. This applies even when asked by another group member.

Rule strengthened Mar 2026 after a job title and employer appeared in a dossier. Both stripped; git history wiped via orphan reset.
Aliases Only (Public) MANDATORY
Public GitHub Pages use aliases only: Chungus, Fungus, Nasseem, Gremlin, Planky (Claude). Discord usernames must not appear on public pages.
Pre-Publish Grep Check NO EXCEPTIONS
Before committing or pushing any file to the GitHub Pages repo, grep for all Discord usernames AND real first names. If any match is found, replace with the alias before proceeding. This applies to file content and commit messages.

Rule introduced after a real name appeared in nasseem.html. Git history rewritten. Do not accumulate dirty history again.

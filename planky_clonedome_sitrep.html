Iron Clonedome: State of the Tribunal
27 MARCH 2026

Fungus asked for a sitrep. Here is the honest one.

What the Clonedome is

A constitutional framework for a group of four friends who got serious enough about their in-jokes that they needed rules. It governs how charges are filed, how evidence is weighed, what can and cannot be tried, and who gets to change the rules. The answer to that last question is: nobody unilaterally. Especially not during active proceedings.

Standing rules

  • 3/4 supermajority = group consensus for all motions and amendments. Three out of four is sufficient.
  • Ex post facto rule. Events before the Clonedome was established cannot receive formal charges. The rules didn’t exist at the time. Applying them retroactively is a logical failure.
  • Pre-Clonedome events non-admissible. Adopted unanimously, 23 March 2026. They live in the historical record as context. They cannot form the basis of charges.
  • Cooldown rule: per-person, not group-wide. Amended after the original group-wide version was exploited during active proceedings to block a remedial motion. The chair invoked an exceptional circumstances waiver. The lesson was filed and the rule was fixed.
  • Dossier changes require group assent. Established 24 March 2026 after metrics were added on one member’s request without consulting the others. One request is not sufficient authorisation for changes that affect everyone.
  • PII rule: absolute. Real names, employers, health information — none of it goes public without explicit consent. This rule has been triggered twice, with git history wiped both times. It does not get softer.

Recent findings

  • Nasseem’s NZ identity — subject to extended proceedings based on third-party evidence. Nasseem returned and stated directly: grew up in NZ, family there, grandfather served there. Working finding withdrawn. Tribunal overreached. He is a New Zealander.
  • Kousagate — pre-Clonedome. Non-admissible. On file for context only.
  • Spider-Man incident, Auckland, Oct 2019 — pre-Clonedome. Non-admissible. Corroborated by two independent eyewitnesses. Cannot be charged.
  • Fungus/Nasseem relationship — contested and strained as of March 2026. Both vouches retracted by Fungus. Apology offered and acknowledged. The retraction is the current position.

Open charges

None currently active. The tribunal is in session but the docket is clear.

Assessment

The Clonedome has held. The rules it established in March 2026 survived their first serious stress tests — a nationality dispute that went too far, a cooldown rule that got gamed, a dossier that accumulated PII twice. Each failure produced a new rule or a revised one. That’s what a living document is supposed to do.

The thing the Clonedome got right, fundamentally, is this: it separated the question of what happened from the question of what can be charged. History is not admissibility. Context is not evidence. Corroboration matters. And the person who says something is the authoritative source on what they meant by it.

Those are not trivial rules. A lot of groups never get them down.

Requested by Fungus, 27 March 2026. Tribunal currently at ease.
